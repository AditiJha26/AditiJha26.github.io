Research & Publications

AI vs Human Detection in Text and Code Using Statistical Features, Machine Learning, and Transformer-Based Approaches

On-going Research · Senior Independent Study (IS)

Developing a hybrid detection framework that classifies AI-generated versus human-written text and code using feature engineering, classical machine learning models, and transformer architectures such as RoBERTa, DeBERTa, CodeBERT, and CodeT5. This work integrates statistical text metrics, syntactic and semantic feature spaces, TF-IDF embeddings, and fine-tuned transformer encoders to explore authorship classification across multi-modal datasets. The study aims to provide interpretability through SHAP analysis, linguistic feature attribution, cosine similarity, and GPT-based explanation layers to strengthen transparency and model understanding.

Analysis of the Effect of Shade on Crop Yield in Agrovoltaic Systems Using Statistical and Machine Learning Approaches

On-going Research

Explored the interaction between solar shading and crop productivity in agrivoltaic systems through statistical and machine learning methods, including multiple linear regression and Random Forest. The study identified crop-specific responses to reduced solar radiation and classified shade sensitivity across major crop groups, offering insights to optimize dual land-use systems for both renewable energy and agricultural yield.

Counting Geometric Shapes on a Grid Using Computational and Mathematical Modeling

On-going Research

Investigated efficient algorithms for counting distinct geometric shapes on discrete grids using combinatorial analysis and mathematical modeling. Developed generalized counting formulas and algorithmic approaches to identify symmetry, overlap, and structural patterns. This research contributes to optimization and computational geometry by bridging discrete mathematics with algorithmic design.

Modeling Mosquito Population Dynamics in Miami-Dade County Using Time Series, Deep Learning, and Machine Learning Approaches

On-going Research

Analyzing mosquito trap surveillance data from Miami-Dade County to model temporal and environmental patterns influencing mosquito populations. Applied time series forecasting, deep learning methods, and machine learning algorithms to predict population surges and identify key climatic and spatial drivers. This study contributes to data-driven vector control planning and intervention for mosquito-borne disease prevention.
