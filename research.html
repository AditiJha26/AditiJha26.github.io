Detecting AI-Generated Text: An Interpretable Multi-Pipeline Analysis Across Stylometric, TF-IDF, and Transformer Representations

On-going Research · Senior Independent Study (IS)

Investigated the problem of distinguishing human-written text from AI-generated text by analyzing how linguistic and representational signals differ across classical machine learning and transformer-based NLP models, with an emphasis on interpretability and evidential reasoning. Designed and evaluated four supervised detection pipelines, including stylometric features with classical ML models, word- and BPE-level TF--IDF representations, fine-tuned transformer models (RoBERTa), and tokenizer-controlled TF--IDF experiments; conducted large-scale training on a curated dataset of 350K English texts using NVIDIA A5000 GPUs. Analyzed learned representations using interpretability methods (SHAP, LIME, PDPs, TF--IDF contribution maps, attention, integrated gradients) and unsupervised techniques (K-means clustering and t-SNE), revealing limited separation for stylometric/TF--IDF features but clear semantic separation in transformer embeddings. Abstract submitted to the National Conference on Undergraduate Research (NCUR), 2026.

Analysis of the Effect of Shade on Crop Yield in Agrivoltaic Systems Using Statistical and Machine Learning Approaches

Explored the interaction between solar shading and crop productivity in agrivoltaic systems through statistical and machine learning methods, including multiple linear regression and Random Forest. The study identified crop-specific responses to reduced solar radiation and classified shade sensitivity across major crop groups, offering insights to optimize dual land-use systems for both renewable energy and agricultural yield. Manuscript finalized for submission to peer-reviewed journals. Cite as:

Greta Heiser, Aditi Jha, Robert Kelvey, et al. Analysis of the Effect of Shade on Crop Yield in Agrivoltaic Systems Using Statistical and Machine Learning Approaches. TechRxiv. December 19, 2025.

Counting Visually Distinct Placements of Possibly Overlapping Axis-Aligned Rectangles on Finite Grids

On-going Research

Investigated efficient algorithms for counting distinct geometric shapes on discrete grids using combinatorial analysis and mathematical modeling. Developed generalized counting formulas and algorithmic approaches to identify symmetry, overlap, and structural patterns. This research contributes to optimization and computational geometry by bridging discrete mathematics with algorithmic design. Abstract submitted to the National Conference on Undergraduate Research (NCUR), 2026.

Modeling Mosquito Population Dynamics in Miami-Dade County Using Time Series, Deep Learning, and Machine Learning Approaches

On-going Research

Analyzing mosquito trap surveillance data from Miami-Dade County to model temporal and environmental patterns influencing mosquito populations. Applied time series forecasting, deep learning methods, and machine learning algorithms to predict population surges and identify key climatic and spatial drivers. This study contributes to data-driven vector control planning and intervention for mosquito-borne disease prevention.
